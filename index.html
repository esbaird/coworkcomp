The Single Blocking Issue

No Audit Log =
No Part 11
Compliance

21 CFR Part 11 §11.10(e) requires complete audit trails for all electronic records. CoWork's local-VM architecture explicitly excludes activity from Anthropic's audit infrastructure. This is a hard regulatory blocker — not a configuration gap.

Until Anthropic ships audit log integration:
CoWork = Tier 1 only · Never Tier 3

Three Regulatory Hard Stops
GxP Compliance
Accuracy · Traceability · Reproducibility
AI agents touching regulated systems become computerized system components requiring validation. LLMs are probabilistic and adaptive — traditional deterministic validation frameworks don't apply.
21 CFR Part 11
§11.10(a)(d)(e)(g)
AI-generated/modified electronic records must have: validation · complete audit trails · authority checks · unique identification of AI operations (not generic admin accounts). CoWork = non-compliant today.
GDPR Art. 9
Purpose Limitation Risk
Agent scheduling a meeting might encounter an attached medical document → creates health-data labels → triggers special-category data rules without consent. EDPS specifically warned about this autonomous data repurposing risk.

Strategic Approach & Next Steps

Five actions to move from assessment to governed enablement.

1
Treat Tools as Complementary, Not Competing
Microsoft Copilot has deep enterprise security integration (Purview, Entra, Defender), making it the stronger candidate for broader deployment and eventual regulated workloads. Claude CoWork currently lacks audit logs and must be strictly limited to Tier 1 non-regulated workflows: research, drafting, and analysis.
2
Target Immediate Tier 1 ROI
Execute a phased approach to capture productivity gains from Tier 1 use cases within 3 to 6 months. During this window, build the governance infrastructure required to eventually support highly regulated Tier 3 workloads.
3
Leverage Existing Takeda Advantages
Takeda is already in a strong starting position compared to peers, thanks to its existing Digital Privacy Officer function, active multi-model AI strategy, and foundation-first data governance approach.
4
Execute Specific Next Actions
Form an AI governance committee, conduct a 21 CFR Part 11 gap assessment, and formally launch the Tier 1 pilot with 50 to 200 users in Q2 2026.
5
Establish a Continuous Review Cadence
Both Anthropic and Microsoft are releasing new features rapidly. Takeda must use its tiered access model alongside a strict review cadence — quarterly access reassessments and vendor capability reviews — to safely expand AI enablement as controls mature.
